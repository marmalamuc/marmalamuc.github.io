Now

Last updated: 02 Apr 2022

Writing

My booksite, The Great Permission, is still in progress, and will be released in 2022.

I’ve been making progress on some modest publicity efforts. I’m still in the planning phase, but have been in contact with a couple professionals in the book publicity space, and now have a rough timeline that would have the booksite online in late summer or early fall.

Publications

My most recent publications are:

  • Two poems in Poetry Salzburg Review
  • Five poems in The Mildred Haun Review
  • In memoriam in The Wild Word
  • Dead Letters (to Brandon) in Stirring
  • What He Didn't Know Before in The West Review

Nothing forthcoming right now! I’ve sent a chapbook called Sour Sea to a handful of places, and applied for some fellowships, but haven’t been submitting for regular journal reading periods. (It’s increasingly difficult to find journals that don’t charge a submission fee.)

Athletics

Cycled off powerlifting for a bit. Still attending no-gi BJJ classes most weeks, but was never able to get the splits right to allow for enough recovery time. Now doing to bodyweight & banded exercises to save my connective tissue.

Learning Spanish

Current Duolingo streak: 1,379 days (as of 02 Apr 2022 )

Currently studying what I suppose we’d call the passive voice in English: Se habla inglés en EEUU. Not sure yet if “passive voice” is the right way to think of this in Spanish.

Other goings on

Travel

I’m visiting Albuquerque soon on another mini-writing retreat to focus on the book. I’m excited to see more of the Southwest, where I’ve only ever been to Phoenix. Second priority is obviously to steal an oh-so fluffy towel from the world-famous Albuquerque Holiday Inn.

Other than a trip or two this summer, I’m also tentatively planning travel to Busan in the fall to visit a good friend who’s likely returning to the U.S. after 14 years in Korea. It’s hard to plan around COVID variants & quarantines; though I could probably work remotely from Korea without anyone really noticing

Professional

Like most knowledge workers, I began working from home during the pandemic. My company has since given most employees a “flex” designation, meaning we have the option to reserve a desk at our office up to two weeks in advance. I like this arrangement, and feel like I’ve settled in to a good cadence. I typically go in one day each week and spend 5–6 very productive, focused hours. The rest of the week is mostly maintenance tasks & meetings.

Perhaps I’ll dig in to that in a future blog post; for now I’d echo the revelations other folks have elaborated on—COVID has revealed there’s little reason to:

  • be physically present at a specific location for work
  • allocate 40 hours per week to work
