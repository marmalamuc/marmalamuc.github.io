Now

Last updated: 24 Nov 2021

I went on a writing retreat last week to push toward completing the book and develop a marketing plan. So of course I started procrastinating immediately by blogging & updating my website.

Writing

I’m very close to done with my first poetry manuscript, The Great Permission—a booksite I’m developing using a custom Jekyll theme that Tom Johnson created. I will release it in 2022.

My blog focuses on my technical journey toward completing the book, which will be available online entirely for free, in perpetuity.

Forthcoming

I have three journal publications forthcoming before the end of 2021:

  • The West Review
  • The Mildred Haun Review out of Walter State Community College
  • Poetry Salzburg Review out of the University of Salzburg

Athletics

My BJJ gym has been drilling takedowns lately, and it turns out I’m very good at leg takedowns. I have long legs, so this probably shouldn’t be surprising. Otherwise, I’m terrible at takedowns (a common disadvantage for jiu-jitsukas), having no real experience with wrestling or other forms of grappling. At the same time, Americanas & Kimuras are becoming my favorite submissions; again, I think my long arms give me a mechanical advantage.

Learning Spanish

Current Duolingo streak: 1,250 days (as of 24 Nov 2021)

My lessons have been focusing on using the preterite & imperfect past tenses in different situations; right now, the construction “[I] just [did] [verb].”

Apparently, there’s a difference between how European Spanish (acabar de [infinitive]) & Latin American Spanish (recién [preterite]) handle this.

Other goings on

1

Did you know Anita Baker covered Tyrese’s Lately?! She absolutely demolishes it. It’s her song now.

2

My partner & I got to attend a wedding celebration for our friends Corey & Brys recently! They got married in Moab, then had a party for friends & family at home in Tennessee. It was wonderful to see people and interact with adults in a non-professional setting.

3

We’re planning for holiday visits. Which is extra complicated now. The relief of seeing family for Thanksgiving & Christmas is tinged with a bit of sadness & anxiety due to the endemic coronavirus.

Both of our families will be in the same area for Christmas, but we each have siblings with newborns & toddlers. So how do we handle visits? Everyone’s vaccinated (like adults who care about other people), but we have to be worried about transmitting the virus to the toddlers (who can’t be vaccinated), the babies (who have no immune system), & the mothers (whose immune systems are still compromised). I understand the reticence—we’re literally talking about the lives of the people we love—I just want to see my family without worrying about whether I’m going to infect them.

4

I made a cheesecake with my mom today, and got her recipe, which will be in the book. It’s famous here in Nashville. If you attended or know someone who attended a Dillon Christmas party (ca. 1995–2008), you’ve probably heard about Jane Dillon’s cheesecake. It’s divine. The best cheesecake there ever was or will be. I am not presently receiving comments on this topic.
