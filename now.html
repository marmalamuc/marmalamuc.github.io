I was recently invited to become a host for Labyrinth’s weekly (Monday) open mic; my first duties are in April.

After almost two years, I’ve finally started submitting to literary journals again. Even though my process is pretty streamlined, it’s still so tedious & frustrating. And even harder to find outlets that don’t charge submission fees.

Upon revisiting the blog entry that details my localhost setup, I notice that it's very out of date. I now use Jekyll exclusively to generate both my websites & my submission documents. The new setup is pretty close to [the one I predicted](/dreamCommonSub#page-level-variables). Although I symlinked the poems to the repos instead of using Git submodules.

Other goings on

At some point recently, Font Awesome updated their library, so my include for icons doesn’t work anymore. I don’t think I’ll restructure my blog or replace the old icons.

Sarah & I visited Eindhoven around NYE, mostly to visit Sprout coffee, which we tasted at last year’s Amsterdam Coffee Festival. We were fortunate to walk in as they were doing a cupping for a small group; after they finished, we were invited to taste. We brought several bags home.

As an Xmas gift, Sarah booked us a spot in a mending & sewing class in January. I’ve wanted to learn for years, but couldn’t convince myself to take the time to figure it out, so it was a wonderful gift.

I finally bought my own bicycle! I had been renting a Swapfiets since November 2022 because I was unwilling to pay for a new bike, but also not inclined to travel around the city test riding a bunch of used bikes from Marktplaats or Facebook Marketplace. Then I became aware of a site called cheapassbikes whose business model is to buy unclaimed bikes from the fietsdepot and refurbish them. The refurbishing was a bit half-assed—I had to get the tires untwisted and replace both sets of brakes—but they reimbursed me. So I have a decent 7-speed city bike for a fraction of what it would have cost new. I’m becoming a real Dutch boy.
