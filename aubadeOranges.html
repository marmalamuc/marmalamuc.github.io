Aubade, with Oranges

I know a woman who says her only talent is the ability to perfectly peel an orange.
She holds it like a Rubik’s Cube, searching for the correct path, before
sinking her thumb into the softest spot and spinning it in gentle orbit
until the rind falls away like garnish for the world’s largest old fashioned.

She tried to teach me her technique. I only tore small chunks,
pressed pulp into my cuticles. In exchange, I tried to explain how a poem
must depart from its inspiration to make a larger (claim) about life,
love, nature, religion. We were both poor teachers.

I remember you, Emily, each time I try to peel an orange.
Each time I smell juniper berries, see the butterfly-shaped
(stain) on my comforter. I remembered you last week,
when, from behind, a woman ran her hands through the loose curls
that hang above the back of my neck, mistaking me for her partner.

You may be (insulted) to know that I also think of you
when (my ex calls). Please believe I don’t associate you two
as one would apples & oranges—

but when you peel an orange, the scent spreads to fill the room.
If you will think of me as the orange, you understand
I have no control over my mind’s {impulsive synapses}
or the range of their diffusion. In one smooth, continuous motion,
you split me open, peeled away the (potential) weighing me down,
(let it spread, evaporate).

for Emily
